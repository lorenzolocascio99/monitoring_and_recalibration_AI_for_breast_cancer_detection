The core problem is this: when an AI system reads a mammogram, it assigns a score (e.g. from 1 to 100), reflecting the likelihood that the patient has cancer. But where do you set the threshold for recall? Too low, and you subject healthy women to unnecessary follow-ups. Too high, and you miss cancers that should have been caught. What makes this especially challenging is that if the system drifts out of calibration (due to a shift in the patient population or a change in imaging equipment) you can't immediately measure the impact on performance. It takes up to 36 months of follow-up to confirm whether a patient was truly healthy or whether a cancer went undetected. My framework addresses this gap by providing a way to flag calibration drift before its consequences become visible in patient outcomes.
